  Harry Potter and the Cursed Child: Part One and Two

  About: This story begins years after the end of Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows novel. This story revolves around the battle between
   Harry's past and the struggle that his children now face. It is full of twists and turns at every page turn.

  Review: I have to admit, at first, I really didn't want to continue reading this novel. I believe the first time I opened it, I got to
   the part where Albus convinces his friend to jump from the Hogwart's Express with him and vanish. I put the book down and cast it from what I enjoy.
   This novel was hard for me, where I felt like I'd been with Harry and his gang growing up through the entire series, but then there is this son of
   Harry's that could treat him this way. It was not in my perfect idolization of Harry Potter books. It did not fit in with the pedestal I put the
   story behind Harry Potter onto. I very must disliked where this book was going, but I also couldn't quit. I'd given this series way too much, having
   reread the series more than once growing up. I would recommend you do not put the novel down, as well. You learn a great deal of true struggles in families.
   It is no longer just about magic, it is about what we outside of this fantasy world could potentially go through when you try to fill your Father's shoes,
   for example. Read on!


  9/10 (for the heartbreak)
